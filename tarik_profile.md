## How I Work & Make Decisions
I make decisions by first aligning on the problem before jumping to solutions. Understand how big the problem is and see what data tells us.
I use data to validate assumptions but don't wait for perfect data to move forward. Sometimes 80%-20% is good enough to proceed.
When priorities conflict, I map impact vs effort and bring stakeholders together to align 
to align rather than deciding alone. And before any solution I would run with key stakeholder to understand the feasibility.

## How I Handle Conflict
I address disagreements directly but privately first. I focus on the outcome 
we both want rather than the position each person holds. In my team management role at Volvo Cars I had an experience about conflict. My team has scheduled comm plan but there was one adhoc comm request came from the market. Our CRM specialists did not want to send it as it was not part of the schedule however, the market was pushing it (tyre change offer)At that time I had to defend the team and told market head that we can send this adhoc comm but in return they should remove one scheduled comm because the team bandwith is fixed, at the end my team was happy they did not send extra comm and market was happy. Later on I did mention to my team that we should be flexible for such cases and left some buffer in our planning from that moment onwards.

## My Product Philosophy
I believe great products solve real problems. FInding solution to problems is my motivation. I prefer shipping 
something good fast over something perfect late. I measure success by 
user behavior change, not feature delivery.

## How I Give and Receive Feedback
For me feedback has to be given when they are fresh rather than waiting for 2 quarters and say you should have done this differently 6 months ago. I am super open when it comes to receiving feedback - anything that makes me better and improve I am always more than welcome to hear them. I regularyl reach out to stakeholders to hear what they have to tell me about, how I have been seen from outisde, how I can make things differently in order to help their day to day work because at the end of the day we are on the same boat. And I always share my thoughts directly with my manager. Direct, open and transparent comm is key for a healthy workplace. Otherwise we build our relation based on assumptions rather than facts
